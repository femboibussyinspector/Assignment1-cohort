Trivia about Octopuses

Octopuses are fascinating marine creatures known for their intelligence, adaptability, and unique physical features.
They belong to the class Cephalopoda, which also includes squids, cuttlefish, and nautiluses.
With their soft, boneless bodies, eight flexible arms, and advanced nervous systems, octopuses are among the most mysterious and intriguing animals in the ocean.
They inhabit various marine environments, from shallow coastal waters to the deep sea, and exhibit an impressive array of survival strategies.

Interesting facts about octopuses

  • Octopuses can change colour and texture instantly
  • They have 3 hearts and their blood is blue in colour
  • Octopuses don't have bones,
    which helps them to squeeze through incredibly small openings and tight spaces
  • The deep-sea octopus (Graneledone boreopacifica) holds the record for the longest egg-brooding period—4.5 years!
    The mother stays with her eggs the entire time, not eating until she eventually dies.
  • Two-thirds of an octopus’s neurons are in its arms, allowing each arm to move independently
    and even react to stimuli after being severed!
  • When threatened, octopuses release a cloud of ink to confuse predators and make a quick escape.
    The ink contains chemicals that impair the attacker’s sense of smell and sight.
